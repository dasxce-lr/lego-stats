Set Value (New/Sealed)

This chart represents the current new/sealed value of the set Detective's Office since March 2018. Very high accuracy
LEGO set 10246 Detective's Office was a 2,262 piece Creator Expert set with 6 minifigs released in January 2015. It is a multi roof line modular set with three buildings and an alley way that includes the main Detective's Office (Ace Brickman) to which the set is named after, a barber's shop, a pool hall, kitchen, bathroom, terrace and an interconnecting section with balconies. In addition, it comes packed with a storyline which is based on the prohibition of sugar, where private detective Ace Brickman is trying to figure out who is smuggling cookies and sweets which is happening right above his office.

It was retired in March 2018 with a lifespan of 38 months. The current value for a new and sealed Detective's Office is estimated around $406 today with current average yearly gain of about 19% but will level off in the next year closer to a 12% annual growth making it a solid investment. On the secondary markets, the typical price range for the set MISB/NISB is between $385 and $500. On the open market, a used set will fetch in the range of between $333 and $420 depending on the condition.

About Icons Modular Buildings

The LEGO ICONS Modular Buildings subtheme is an extension of the Advanced Models theme. Modular Buildings features large, detailed, connectable buildings, aimed at adults and teenagers.

Modular Buildings currently consists of 10 sets that as of now has run from 2013 to 2022. As of today, 10251 Brick Bank is the most valuable Modular Buildings set with an estimated new/factory sealed value of $569.
Summary
Icons Modular Buildings Detective's Office set 10246 was released in January 2015, retailing for $160, but has since been retired. New, unopened Detective's Office sets are valued at $406, while used sets can be found for $350 in good condition.

Discover a world of mystery and adventure with the LEGO® Creator Expert Detective’s Office! Step through the open archway and into the barbershop, where seated customers are pampered in the reflection of a large wall mirror, while next door, competitors play pool and darts beneath the comforting whir of a rotating ceiling fan. Venture to the first floor and you’ll find the detective’s office, his desk strewn with clues, a safe containing valuable evidence and a concealed wall compartment. Then visit the adjacent bathroom, featuring a classic pull-chain toilet, before taking the stairway to the well-equipped kitchen, from where you can access the roof terrace, complete with large water tower.
